SCA | Quick News | March 25, 2022

Skoden in the news

The 2022 Skoden Indigenous Film Festival (SIFF) was covered in The Georgia Straight! Read all about it HERE.

There are two ways to see the festival. One is to catch the Skoden Sampler on March 31, 2022, at the Prospera Credit Union Theatre at SFU's Surrey Campus (13450 102 Ave Unit 110, Surrey), and the other is to catch the complete Skoden Indigenous Film Festival run on April 1 & 2, 2022, at the Djavad Mowafaghian Cinema here at SFU Goldcorp Centre for the Arts (149 W. Hastings St., Vancouver). But if you're really keen, you could go to everything! Grab sliding-scale tickets for both the Skoden Sampler and / or the full Skoden Indigenous Film Festival here: Get tickets! NOTE: Please make sure to purchase a ticket for each program for the full Skoden Indigenous Film Festival on April 1 & 2 you'd like to attend. Head over to the dedicated 2022 Skoden Indigenous Film Festival page for the complete program and information about the films in the festival. It's going to be great!

Architek Percussion sell out!

Music on Main's presentation of Architek Percussion on Tuesday, March 29, 2022, featuring a "world premiere of an epic new piece" titled Stircrazer I by the SCA's Sabrina Schroeder (who's also Music on Main's Composer in Residence), is SOLD OUT! Let's hope some video from the event gets shared for folks who missed a ticket. More HERE.

Daniel talks Fitting (Out-Fitting) In

The SCA's Dr. Henry Daniel is giving a talk, Fitting (Out-Fitting) In, for the Centre for Culture, Identity and Education at UBC, on Tuesday, March 29, 2022, from 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM, drawing from his essay with the same title from the book Nuances of Blackness in the Canadian Academy: Teaching, Learning, and Researching While Black (University of Toronto Press, 2022), edited by Awad Ibrahim, Tamari Kitossa, Malinda S. Smith, and Handel Kashope Wright. RSVP HERE.

Andreyev and Overstall's EPIC_Tom ++

The SCA's Stefan Smulovitz and SCA PhD student Simon Overstall are part of the ace group of players (also including Julie Andreyev, Adrian Avendaño, Marina Hasselberg, Giorgio Magnanensi, and Anju Singh) performing Andreyev and Overstall's EPIC_Tom ++, which "features three works of live musical improvisation and generative media from Julie Andreyev and Simon Overstall’s multispecies projects with dogs, crows, and forest communities." Co-presented by Vancouver New Music and Emily Carr University of Art + Design, EPIC_Tom ++ runs on Saturday, May 7, 2022, at 7:30 PM at ECU's Integrated Motion Studio (520 E 1st Avenue, enter from Carolina Street). More HERE.
