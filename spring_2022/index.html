AHVA Visual Resources Centre, Spring 2022 Newsletter

PixPlot

We are testing out a new visualization system for large batches of images, called PixPlot, which utilizes artificial intelligence to analyze, categorize, and display thousands of images in a 2D or 3D space. Come check it out. We hope to use PixPlot to eventually demonstrate a digital form of Aby Warburg’s Bilderatlas Mnemosyne. We continue to explore new digital tools that are useful for both pedagogical and research purposes. Please do come by to discuss your needs, and explore how we can help.

NFT and Blockchain update

On March 9 we hosted a roundtable discussion on “NFTs, Crypto Art, and Digital Collections?” with Rémi Castonguay, Timothy Fernandes, and AHVA MA Alumna Blake Finucane. Attended by 80 people on Zoom, the roundtable participants discussed the economic, historical, technological, and preservation aspects of this new art form. The VRC is particularly interested in the process and method of collecting and archiving a medium that is inherently unstable and difficult to contain. We see this process as part of the long-history of the VRC's interest in staying abreast of new technologies and developments in art and media.

Slide deaccessioning continues

Deaccessioning of slides is ongoing. Thirty or so students have visited to pick up slides—some take a few, others take dozens. It is a slow process but has been a good point of contact to interact with students and introduce them to the VRC.

spaces and services survey results

The VRC Spaces and Services Survey has closed. We received 47 responses—thank you!—and we are now processing the results. More to come shortly. In the last two weeks we also held two focus groups regarding the space; one with graduate students and one with undergraduates. We will soon hold a focus group with faculty.

drop-in writing clinic for undergraduates 

The writing clinic for undergraduates has finished for the term. We will resume the clinic in the fall with set regular hours.

gis workshop and tools

On March 18 we held a workshop on GIS (Geographic Information Systems) with Jeff O’Brien and UBC GIS librarian Evan Thornberry, which looked at the uses of GIS technology in art historical practices and research. We are currently using CollectionBuilder for several projects, including Joseph Monteyne's ARTH 322 class, and have assisted several builds of the software for undergraduate and graduate student projects.    

reading nook

As part of the ongoing decluttering and slide deaccessioning process we have cleared away a small corner that is now a nook for reading and research. Open to all. Is also a good spot for office hours.  

spring departures

We want to note that this term is Alexi Paglinawan’s last term as a work-learn student in the VRC as she is focusing on finishing her MA thesis. Thank you, Alexi!
