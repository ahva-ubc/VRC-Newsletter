AHVA Visual Resources Centre, Fall 2022 Newsletter

We start the academic year welcoming a relative return to normal and more in-person interactions in our renewed Visual Resources Centre. In anticipation of the 2022 Winter session, a number of AHVA staff worked to refresh the VRC space. Slide cabinets were moved aside and redundant furnishing removed to create a more open and flexible floorplan. We have plenty of space for faculty and students to work from. And new plants! As always, our team is here to help you with any research and teaching requests, whether they involve troubleshooting AV issues, scanning high-res images, or supporting a digital art history project (service request form).

digital humanities event: AI-assisted interfaces for exploring large-scale visual collections

Join us on November 3 at 5:30 pm in Lasserre 102 for a presentation by Lindsay King (Head Librarian, Bowes Art & Architecture Library, Stanford University) and Peter Leonard (Assistant University Librarian for Research Data Services, Stanford University). Their talk, entitled “AI-assisted Interfaces for Exploring Large-scale Visual Collections” will look at alternative modes of image discovery and access. Libraries, archives, and museums have spent the past few decades building and curating massive digital collections–how can new tools give us new perspectives on what we have? In this talk, we will discuss using AI tools for exploring digital image collections from cultural heritage institutions. In some cases, AI tools can help us visualize images and their metadata differently, or work around a lack of metadata by sorting according to image similarity. We aim to create discovery platforms and entry points rather than classifiers or taggers. The potential in these interfaces is for AI to assist human viewers in seeing new patterns and similarities.

book scanner

Stop by to use our new! fast! book scanner that is capable of scanning one page per second. This scanner has practically everything you may need, including book curve flattening technology, optical character recognition, auto-alignment, auto-scan, and more. You can laser scan an entire book in just minutes. Come try it out and we’ll give you a tour.
