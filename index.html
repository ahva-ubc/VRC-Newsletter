Curator Notes

It has been an intense few months since I started working at AHVA! Over the summer, with the help of Jane Young, we re-organized the VRC and got rid of old equipment and supplies we kept some really cool retro stuff too!) We created a couple new meeting spaces for students and faculty to use. For a full report please download our Winter I Report here. As our lives slowly start going back to normal please come visit us and use our space and services! Aside from the VRC's selection of dongles and laptops ready to be checked out, there are tables and seating available for quick study sessions or meetings. The array of slide drawers and books that have been part of the VRC's long history are also at one's disposal for casual viewing or research. Keep on reading, we have a number of exciting news items below!

MORE STAFFING HIGHLIGHTS!

Alexi Paglinawan


Alexi Paglinawan is a second year MA student of Art History focusing on Philippine colonial art under imperial Spain. She has been working in the VRC since October of 2020 and, although you might not have seen her around due to the pandemic, she has been an important part of the team for her help in cataloguing and weeding out photograph slides in great numbers. With the academic year in full swing, she hopes to see more people come into the VRC to explore and utilize the space, as well as take advantage of the VRC's services 

Richard Chapplow


Richard Chapplow received his BFA, concentrating in ceramics from Emily Carr University of Art + Design in 2016, a Diploma of Art History from UBC in 2018, and an M.A. from UBC in 2021 in which he examined the materiality of paint and its role in the generation of affect, primarily in paintings of animal flesh and meat. Richard is currently pursuing a PhD in Art History, focusing on how the human / animal debate of the 17th C played out within early modern painting and print. A lifelong geek and metalhead, when not entrenched within academic pursuits, Richard can be found learning doom and sludge metal basslines, building guitar effects pedals, and building mechanical keyboards.

Jessica Law


Jessica Law will be a familiar figure to most of you as she is wearing many hats at AHVA. One of those roles is being a Graduate Academic Assistant (GAA). As such she is focused on helping faculty teach online and has been compiling and documenting resources avaiable for professors. This term she continues in her role and will also assist the VRC by compiling additional resources that can help faculty guide students in the research process (e.g., pointing to appropriate library databases, or providing paper writing guidance, etc.)

Jess Yao 


Jess Yao is a Master of Library and Information Studies student at the UBC School of Information with a background in English and Classics. Her research interests include critical information literacy, research dissemination, and social justice issues in information organizations. In her professional experience course at the VRC this term, She hopes to help strengthen and share about research data management (RDM) practices for AHVA faculty, with a focus on managing visual and other sources. She is looking forward to getting to know you!

Copyrights in the Visual Arts: a Q&A Session

Artists and art historians have a particular interest in copyrights issues and how it might interact with their practice or research. In this session we will try to answer your most pressing questions!

Creating Digital Exhibits: A Survey of Tools

This workshop will cover an assortment of digital exhibit and storytelling tools such as Omeka, Scalar, static site generators (CollectionBuilder and Wax), and narrative tools such as Timeline.js and Storymaps from Knight Lab.

 

Learn More

Research Data Management: Principles and Tools

In this workshop we will lay out basic principles of Research Data Management (RDM): how to document your research appropriately, keep track of file version.  We'll then talk about a few tools (e.g., Tropy) that can help the researcher manage digital assets.
