MORE STAFFING HIGHLIGHTS!

Alexi Paglinawan


Alexi Paglinawan is a secon- year MA student of Art History focusing on Philippine colonial art under imperial Spain. She has been working in the VRC since October of 2020 and, although you might not have seen her around due to the pandemic, she has been an important part of the team for her help in cataloguing and weeding out photograph slides in great numbers. With the academic year in full swing, she hopes to see more people come into the VRC to explore and utilize the space, as well as take advantage of the VRC's services 

Richard Chapplow


Richard Chapplow received his BFA, concentrating in ceramics from Emily Carr University of Art + Design in 2016, a Diploma of Art History from UBC in 2018, and an MA from UBC in 2021 in which he examined the materiality of paint and its role in the generation of affect, primarily in paintings of animal flesh and meat. Richard is currently pursuing a PhD in Art History, focusing on how the human/animal debate of the 17th C played out within early modern painting and print. A lifelong geek and metalhead, when not entrenched within academic pursuits, Richard can be found learning doom and sludge metal basslines, building guitar effects pedals, and building mechanical keyboards.

Jessica Law


Jessica Law will be a familiar figure to most of you as she is wearing many hats at AHVA. One of those roles is being a Graduate Academic Assistant (GAA). As such she is focused on helping faculty teach online and has been compiling and documenting resources available for professors. This term she continues in her role and will also assisSt the VRC by compiling additional resources that can help faculty guide students in the research process (e.g., pointing to appropriate library databases, or providing paper writing guidance, etc.)

Jess Yao 


Jess Yao is a Master of Library and Information Studies student at the UBC School of Information with a background in English and Classics. Her research interests include critical information literacy, research dissemination, and social justice issues in information organizations. In her professional experience course at the VRC this term, she hopes to help strengthen and share about research data management (RDM) practices for AHVA faculty, with a focus on managing visual and other sources. She is looking forward to getting to know you!
