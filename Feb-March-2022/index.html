SLIDES DEACCESSION PROJECT
We are currently reviewing our post-1945 collection, which is significant and contains close to 23,000 images. All of these works are available on PIX, the VRC's online database.  (See https://ahva-vrc.adc.arts.ubc.ca.) As such we no longer have any use for most of the physical slides and are looking to dispose of them. Or… perhaps you’d like to use slides for an art project? AHVA memento? Drop by the VRC in Lasserre 206 between 9am-5pm, to grab as many slides as you like. Or email us at ahva.vrc@ubc.ca.

WORKSHOPS & EVENTS

NFTs, Crypto Art, and Digital Collections? - A Roundtable Discussion

NFTs have taken the visual arts by storm in the past few years. This roundtable, moderated by Prof. T'ai Smith, will take a look at economic, historical, technological and preservation aspects of this new art form. More details forthcoming via the AHVA website. 

When: March 9 @ 5:30-7pm

Where: Register on Zoom

Image credits: Yuga Labs (https://boredapeyachtclub.com)

Voyant for the Art Visual Arts

This workshop will provide an introduction to principles of text analysi , a subfield of the Digital Humanities. We will use Voyant (https://voyant-tools.org) to analyze a small corpus of seminal art-related writings from the past.

When: March 11 @ 10-11:30am

Where: Register on Zoom

Graphic Information System (GIS) for the Visual Arts

This workshop will explore the use of GIS-based software to inform art historians and visual artists about their potential application in scholarship and practice. We'll take a look at a few tools but the primary goal is to introduce participants to GIS as a "way of seeing".

When: March 18th @ 10am-11:30am

Where: Register on Zoom

News from the Library

Have you ever looked at Library Stack? The Library has a 1-year subscription that began on September 1, 2021.The Library Stack interface offers a compelling and visual way to browse information.

  • This resource provides access to born-digital image, text, and audio files for contemporary art, design, architecture, media studies, cinema, and philosophy.
  • Select publishers in this database include: Sternberg Press, Aperture Foundation, Hatje Cantz, and e-flux, among others.
  • Users are prompted to create an account to access resources. Digital resources have embed codes, so content can be added directly into a Canvas course module for easy access and reference.

Writing Clinic﻿

The VRC will launch a Writing Clinic in March   

The VRC, in collaboration with Profs. Rachel Boate and Julia Orell, will launch a Research & Writing Clinic in early March for undergraduate students.  The clinic will provide critical and constructive commentary on the structure of students' papers, including assistance with the thesis statement and argument. To help students access library resources, the VRC will also collaborate with our Art and Visual Literacy Librarian, Sara Ellis. Please contact us if you have questions about this service: ahva.vrc@ubc.ca
