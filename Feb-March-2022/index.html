TERM 2 - WORKSHOPS

NFTs, Crypto Art, and Digital Collections? - A Roundtable Discussion

NFTs have taken the visual arts by storm in the last few years. This roundtable moderated by Prof. T'ai Smith will take a look at economic, historical, technological and preservation aspects of this new art form.

When: March 11th @ 10am-11:30am

Where: For now, register on Zoom

GIS for the Art Visual Arts

This workshop will provide an introduction to text analysis principles, a subfield of the Digital Humanities. We will use Voyant (https://voyant-tools.org) to analyze a small corpus of seminal art-related writings from the past.

When: March 18th @ 10am-11:30am

Where: For no, register on Zoom

Voyant for the Visual Arts

This workshop will explore the use of GIS-based software to inform art historians and visual artists about their potential application in scholarship and practice. We'll take a look at a few tools but the primary goal is to introduce participants to GIS as a "way of seeing".

When: March 18th @ 10am-11:30am

Where: For no, register on Zoom

News From the Library

Have you ever looked at Library Stack? The Library has a 1-year subscription that began on September 1, 2021.The Library Stack interface offers a very compelling and visual way to browse through information.

  • This resource provides access to born-digital image, text, and audio files for contemporary art, design, architecture, media studies, cinema, and philosophy.
  • Select publishers in this database include: Sternberg Press, Aperture Foundation, Hatje Cantz, and e-flux, among others.
  • Users are prompted to create an account to access resources. Digital resources have embed codes, so content can be added directly into a Canvas course module for easy access and reference.

Research Data Management: Principles and Tools

In this workshop we will lay out basic principles of Research Data Management (RDM): how to document your research appropriately, keep track of file version.  We'll then talk about a few tools (e.g., Tropy) that can help the researcher manage digital assets.

 

December 10, 2021, 11am-12pm on Zoom
